Introduction

Mr Mrinal Saharay is a Consultant General Surgeon with extensive knowledge in endocrine and colorectal surgery.

Mr Saharay has over 30 years experience practicing as a Consultant, serving as Clinical Lead of Thyroid Surgery at Queen's Hospital, Romford, and significantly advancing his chosen sub-specilities, becoming a fellow of the Royal College of Surgeons of England.

—

Specialism

Mr Saharay specialises in endocrine and colorectal surgery.

Mr Saharay's endocrine experience encompasses all thyroid and parathyroid procedures, including those for cancers as well as minimally invasive parathyroid surgery. In addition, he performs a wide range of colonic operations for both cancers and benign conditions. He has a particular interest in the treatment of haemorrhoids and performs the latest operation for this condition called the Procedure for Prolapse and Haemorrhoids (PPH). In addition he performs gastroscopy and colonoscopy, along with other general operations such as hernia repairs and laparoscopic gall bladder surgery.

—

Experience

Mr Saharay has decades of experience in his field.

Mr Saharay’s initial medical training was carried out in India, with his higher surgical training completed in the North East Thames area. As clinical lead for thyroid surgery at Queens Hospital, Romford, and network lead for the North East Thames Cancer Network, he has been responsible for introducing improvements to the service and advancing the sub-specialty considerably. He has worked closely with the endocrinology department at Queens, undertaking joint clinics to provide the best possible care. Mr Saharay is a member of The British Association of Endocrine Surgeons and The British Association of Colorectal Surgeons, taking an active part in their annual conferences.

Practice

Mr Saharay practices at Queen's Hospital Romford, and at private hospitals across Essex.

Mr Saharay’s NHS practice is at Queen’s Hospital Romford and he is also available for consultation in various private hospitals in Essex.
